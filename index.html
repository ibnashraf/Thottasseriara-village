About Thottasseriara

Our History

Thottasseriara is a picturesque village with a rich history dating back centuries. The name "Thottasseriara" is believed to have originated from [explain origin of name]. Our village has maintained its traditional charm while embracing modern development.

The village has a population of approximately 6000 people, primarily engaged in Agriculture. The cultural fabric of Thottasseriara is woven with traditions of Onam, Christmas, Bakrreed, etc

Cultural Background

Thottasseriara is known for its vibrant cultural heritage. The village celebrates various festivals throughout the year, including Onam Eid Christmas. Traditional art forms are also preserved and practiced by the villagers.

Tourist and Visitor Guide

How to Reach

By Road

Thottasseriara is well connected by road. The nearest major town is Kondotty, located 10 km away. Regular bus services are available from Tottasseriara Town.

By Rail

The nearest railway station is Parappanangadi, approximately 20 km from the village. Auto rickshaws and taxis are available from the station.

By Air

Caliicut airport is the nearest airport, about 15 km from Thottasseriara. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the village.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Thottasseriara is between November and December when the weather is pleasant. The village festival during September is a particularly vibrant time to experience local culture.

Local Food

Don't miss trying our local specialties like Ghee-Roast, Biriyani, and Porotta & Beef-fry from The Nihmath hotel, is highly recommended for authentic village cuisine.

Places to Stay

While Thottasseriara has limited lodging options, homestays are available with local families. Nearby Kunnumpuram offers hotels ranging from budget to mid-range.
